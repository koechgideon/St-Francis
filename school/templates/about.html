St. Francis Children's Village

The Children's Village is the inspiration of Fr. Francis Limo Riwa who in 1999 began to rescue orphaned and abandoned children from the streets. Today, it is home, family and school to more than 600 children, living on two campuses: the St. Clare Girls’ Center and the St. Francis Home for Boys. It is a safe haven that provides quality education and rehabilitative efforts for deterring children from destructive addictive behaviors often learned from the place they call home.

Children are exposed to good health care, hygiene, nutrition, and education while acquiring tools for proper social behavior. At the end of the introductory period, it is then that they join the other children of The Village, while furthering their education capabilities. Teachers take on the role of parents and previous neglected children now have a sense of family.
